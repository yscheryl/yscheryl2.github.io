Data Visualization

Data source: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/paakhim10/taylor-swift-the-myth-the-legend?resource=download

Choice of data visualization

Characteristics of albums

The dataset presented provided a number of music characteristics, of which I chose 3 defining characteristics to visualize her music.

  1. Danceability: How suitable a track is for dancing based on a combination of musical elements including tempo, rhythm stability, beat strength, and overall regularity. Quantified a scale of 0.0 (least danceable) to 1.0 (most danceable).
  1. Tempo: The speed or pace of a given piece. Quantified through beats per minute (BPM).
  1. Acousticness: A confidence measure of whether the track is acoustic. Quantified on a scale of 0 (low confidence that the track is acoustic) to 1 (high confidence that the track is acoustic).

Popularity of albums

The dataset presented provided the popularity of each song in her discography, quantified on a scale of 0 (least popular) to 100 (most popular). As each song was tagged to an album, a box plot is useful to show how the popularity data is distributed within each album, and if there are any outliers.

Findings from data visualization

Danceability

There is some variability in the albums’ danceability, with the highest danceability being 1989 (Taylor’s Version), followed by Midnights (Til The Dawn Edition), and Lover. On the other hand, Taylor Swift had the lowest level of danceability, followed by evermore (deluxe version) and folklore (deluxe version).

Acousticness

There is great variability in the albums’ acousticness, with evermore (deluxe version) having the highest level of acousticness, followed by folklore (deluxe version) and Midnights (Til The Dawn Edition). Meanwhile, the rest of the albums exhibited lower levels of acousticness, especially that of 1989 (Taylor’s version), Red (Taylor’s Version), reputation, and Taylor Swift.

Tempo

All albums have relatively high levels of tempo, with the highest being Fearless (Taylor’s Version) and the lowest being Taylor Swift.

Popularity

There is some variation in the popularity of each album. reputation has the highest median popularity, followed by Lover, while Taylor Swift has a much lower median popularity. The interquartile range is the highest in Lover, reputation, and Red (Taylor’s version), and the lowest in folklore (deluxe edition) and evermore (deluxe edition). Some of the albums have outliers, including evermore (deluxe version), Fearless (Taylor’s Version), Lover, and Taylor Swift.

Analysis of data visualization

Danceability and Popularity

Albums with higher median popularity, such as reputation and Lover, tend to have higher levels of danceability, while albums with lower median popularity, such as Taylor Swift, have lower danceability. This could be because music of higher danceability levels appeal to a broader audeince, while those of lower danceability levels are more niche and are less mainstream amongst listeners.

Acousticness and Popularity

Albums with a smaller interquartile range of popularity, such as folklore (deluxe version) and evermore (deluxe version), score higher in terms of level of acousticness. On the other hand, albums with a higher interquartile range of popularity, such as reputation and Red (Taylor’s Version), have lower levels of acousticness. A potential explanation is that different music of higher levels of acousticness tend to be enjoyed by the same audience due to the consistency in style, leading to a smaller interquartile range of popularity (i.e. the popularity of the songs do not vary so much). Meanwhile, music of lower levels of acousticness are not as consistent in how much they appeal to audiences, thus resulting in higher interquartile range of popularity (i.e. the popularity of the songs vary a lot).

Overall

Overall, the interplay between the characteristics of Swift’s music and their popularity is very complex, and while characteristics like danceability, acousticness, and tempo may influence the appeal and popularity of the album, they are not the sole determining factors, as other considerations, such as marketing strategies, time of release, and cultural relevance play a huge part as well. Nonetheless, it is important and interesting to consider how there may be some correlation between these characteristics and the popularity of her music.
